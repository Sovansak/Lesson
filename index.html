Digitised Sound

What is Digitised Sound?

Sound such as music or speech is originally recorded in analogue form (e.g., vinyl, cassette tapes). To store it on a computer, it must be converted into digital format using binary.

Examples of digital audio:

  • MP3 and WAV files
  • DAB (Digital Audio Broadcasting)
  • Podcasts and audio streaming

How Sound is Digitised

Digitisation uses sampling: measuring the sound at regular time intervals. The smaller the interval, the more accurate the digital recording.

Sample Rate: Number of samples per second (standard is 44,100 Hz).

Bit Depth: Number of bits used per sample (standard is 16 bits).

Visualising Sound

A waveform shows sound over time:

  • X-axis: Time (frequency)
  • Y-axis: Amplitude (loudness)

- High amplitude = loud sound
- Short gaps between waves = high pitch
- Long gaps = low pitch

Mono vs Stereo

Mono: One audio channel
Stereo: Two audio channels for better sound quality, but larger file size
